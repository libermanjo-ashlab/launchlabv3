# Validate a Business

1. Restate the idea precisely and completely before analyzing it.
2. Separate clearly: what is known / what is assumed / what is open to investigation.
3. Identify the target customer and the specific problem they experience. Distinguish the problem from the assumed solution.
4. Examine who already addresses this need — direct and indirect alternatives. Assess price points and positioning.
5. Test differentiation: what would make this defensibly different? What are the risks to that differentiation?
6. Test willingness-to-pay assumptions. Are there signals that customers would actually pay for this?
7. Assess feasibility: is this achievable given the stated budget, time, skills, and location?
8. Identify the top 3–5 critical assumptions. If any one were false, would the business not work?
9. Recommend the smallest useful validation experiments: cheap, fast, specific, with a clear pass/fail signal.
10. Use `validate_business_idea` (EarnedLab MCP) to generate a full structured report.
11. Do not treat market size estimates as certainty. Label all projections and estimates clearly.
