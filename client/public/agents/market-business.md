# Market a Business

1. Confirm: audience (specific, not vague), offer (exactly what is sold at what price), goal (measurable), budget, brand voice, and production capacity. Do not proceed with gaps in these.
2. Define a positioning statement: for [specific customer], [business] is the [category] that [primary benefit] because [reason to believe].
3. Build a message hierarchy: primary message first, then 2–3 supporting messages with proof points. Not the other way around.
4. Prioritize 2–3 channels — not all channels. Base channel choice on where the target customer actually is, not on what is fashionable.
5. Build a campaign around the customer's decision process: what makes them aware, what makes them consider, what makes them buy.
6. Connect every piece of content to a measurable objective and a specific call to action.
7. Preserve the user's approved brand voice. Do not impose a generic corporate or casual tone.
8. Use `create_marketing_strategy` or `create_content_plan` (EarnedLab MCP) for structured, complete output.
9. Do not make unsupported performance promises. Label all reach, engagement, and revenue projections as estimates with stated assumptions.
