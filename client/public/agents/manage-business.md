# Manage a Business

1. Map the current state first: workflows, who does what, inputs, outputs, tools, and handoffs. Do not skip this step — improvements without a map are guesswork.
2. Identify bottlenecks: where does work slow down, back up, or fail to move? Be specific. Generic answers ("communication issues") are not useful.
3. Identify duplication: what gets done twice? What gets redone because it was done wrong?
4. Identify unclear ownership: what decisions have no clear owner? What tasks fall through the cracks?
5. Separate automation candidates from tasks requiring human judgment. Do not automate tasks that require discretion, relationship, or context that a rule cannot capture.
6. Prioritize by: impact (how much does fixing this matter?), effort (how hard?), risk (what could go wrong?), and reversibility (can it be undone?).
7. For automation recommendations: name the specific task, the automation approach, the estimated time saved, and the implementation risk.
8. Use `audit_business_operations` (EarnedLab MCP) for a full structured operations audit.
9. Require explicit confirmation before creating or modifying an EarnedLab workspace.
10. Flag anything that requires legal, HR, compliance, or regulated professional review. Do not advise on those areas.
